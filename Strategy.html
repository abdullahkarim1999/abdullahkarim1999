Value Investing

This type of investing focuses on something called Fundamental Analysis. This sort of analysis is considered "old school", as it has to do with how investors such as Warren Buffett and Ray Dalio may have invested into markets. Value investing focuses on essentially five ideas:

  • The Intrinsic Value of Companies
  • Margins of Safety
  • Market Efficiency is a Myth
  • Herding is Bad
  • Patience is Key

The idea behind these five fundamentals is that if you follow them, you will have a set strategy that will work basically forever. The idea is to look into a company to see if it has a good reputation, make sure their mission is clear and great, and to see if they are being sold at a good price.
